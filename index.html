Revelation Chapter 1

Chapter Overview: Revelation chapter 1 introduces the book as the revelation of Jesus Christ given to His servant John, establishing the prophetic timeline and the identity of the promised pastor. In the Shincheonji reality (실상), this chapter describes the calling of the new John (Lee Man-hee) in the era of betrayal (배도) after the fall of the 7 messengers of Jangmak Seongjeon in 1967. It sets the stage for the physical fulfillment of all prophetic events, beginning with the commissioning of the promised pastor who will testify to everything he sees and hears.

Verse 1

The revelation of Jesus Christ, which God gave him to show to his servants the things that must soon take place. He made it known by sending his angel to his servant John.

Literal meaning: This is the unveiling of Jesus Christ, given by God the Father to reveal future events to His servants. SCJ reality (실상): This refers to the prophecy being physically revealed in the end times through the promised pastor. The angel sent is a messenger from heaven who delivers the revelation to the new John (Lee Man-hee), appointing him as the recorder of the physical fulfillment. The "servants" are those who will be warned and saved through the testimony. Cross-references: Dan 2:28 – God reveals what must be in the latter days; Rev 22:6 – The Lord sends His angel to show His servants.

Verse 2

who bore witness to the word of God and to the testimony of Jesus Christ, even to all that he saw.

Literal meaning: John testifies that he faithfully recorded everything he saw and heard from the angel. SCJ reality (실상): The new John (Lee Man-hee) bears witness to the revealed word of God and the testimony of Jesus Christ as it is physically fulfilled in Korea. He saw the events of betrayal, destruction, and salvation with his own eyes and recorded them for future generations. Cross-references: 1 John 1:1 – That which we have seen and heard we proclaim; Rev 19:10 – The testimony of Jesus is the spirit of prophecy.

Verse 3

Blessed is the one who reads aloud the words of this prophecy, and blessed are those who hear, and who keep what is written in it, for the time is near.

Literal meaning: A blessing is promised to those who read, hear, and keep the prophecy. SCJ reality (실상): This blessing is for those who understand the physical fulfillment of Revelation as testified by the new John. The "time is near" refers to the end times when the prophecy is being fulfilled. Those who hear the testimony of Shincheonji and keep its teachings—that is, hold fast to the physical reality of the revelation—will receive salvation. Cross-references: Luke 11:28 – Blessed are those who hear the word of God and keep it; Rev 22:7 – Blessed is the one who keeps the words of the prophecy.

Verse 4

John to the seven churches that are in Asia: Grace to you and peace from him who is and who was and who is to come, and from the seven spirits who are before his throne.

Literal meaning: John addresses the seven literal churches in Asia with a greeting of grace and peace from the eternal God and the seven spirits. SCJ reality (실상): The "seven churches" here represent the seven messengers of Jangmak Seongjeon, the tabernacle tent pastor tribes that were established after 1967. "Asia" spiritually points to the location of these churches in Korea. The "seven spirits" are the complete spiritual enablement given to these messengers, but they later fell into betrayal. Cross-references: Zech 4:2 – The seven lamps are the seven eyes of the Lord; Rev 3:1 – The seven spirits of God.

Literal meaning: The greeting includes Jesus Christ as the faithful witness, the first to rise from the dead, and the ruler over earthly kings. SCJ reality (실상): Jesus Christ is the faithful witness whose testimony is fulfilled in the physical reality. The "firstborn of the dead" points to Jesus' resurrection, but in the end times, the new John becomes the witness of the first resurrection (Rev 20). Jesus freed believers from sin through His blood, and the fulfillment of this redemption is realized only through those who are sealed and saved in the physical events. Cross-references: Col 1:18 – He is the firstborn from the dead; 1 Pet 1:18-19 – Redeemed with the precious blood of Christ.

Literal meaning: Believers are made a kingdom of priests to serve God. SCJ reality (실상): This is fulfilled in the 12 tribes of Shincheonji, where the 144,000 sealed members become a royal priesthood. The "messengers" (angelos) are the seven pastors of the physical Tabernacle Temple (Jangmak Seongjeon), who fell into betrayal after 1967. This chapter covers the first four churches (Ephesus, Smyrna, Pergamum, Thyatira), which SCJ identifies with the early stages of betrayal, persecution, and the rise of the Nicolaitan heresy (Cheongji Kyoyukwon). Key dates center on the period after the promised pastor's calling (Rev 1) and before the heavenly throne scene (Rev 4).

"To the angel of the church in Ephesus write: 'The words of him who holds the seven stars in his right hand, who walks among the seven golden lampstands.'" (ESV)

Literal meaning: Jesus addresses the messenger (angel) of the Ephesian church, identifying Himself as the one who controls the seven stars (messengers) and moves among the lampstands (churches). SCJ reality (실상): The "angel of Ephesus" is the first of the seven physical pastors of Jangmak Seongjeon, who received the promised word. The "seven stars" are these seven pastors (Rev 1:20), and the "lampstands" are the seven churches they led. This verse calls the Ephesus-era pastor to remember his original duty, before betrayal began. Cross-references: Rev 1:20; Matt 5:14-16.

Verse 2

"I know your works, your toil and your patient endurance, and how you cannot bear with those who are evil, but have tested those who call themselves apostles and are not, and found them to be false." (ESV)

Literal meaning: Jesus commends the Ephesian church for their hard work, perseverance, and discernment against false apostles. SCJ reality (실상): In the early period (1967–1970s), the first pastor of Jangmak Seongjeon faithfully tested those claiming to be "apostles" — referring to the Nicolaitan false teachers who later took over Cheongji Kyoyukwon. The physical work included building the Tabernacle Temple and enduring persecution from established denominations. Cross-references: 2 Cor 11:13; 1 John 4:1.

Verse 3

"I know you are enduring patiently and bearing up for my name's sake, and you have not grown weary." (ESV)

Literal meaning: Jesus acknowledges their steadfast endurance despite hardship. SCJ reality (실상): The Ephesus-era pastor and his followers endured physical persecution from both secular authorities and religious leaders, but did not waver in their faith in the promised pastor, Lee Man-hee, who was then being prepared. Cross-references: Heb 12:1; 2 Thess 3:13.

Verse 4

"But I have this against you, that you have abandoned the love you had at first." (ESV)

Literal meaning: Jesus rebukes them for losing their initial fervent love. SCJ reality (실상): By the late 1970s, the Ephesus pastor began to cool in his devotion to the true word, gradually shifting his allegiance to the Nicolaitan system of Cheongji Kyoyukwon, which sought worldly power. The "first love" is the original revelation received from the promised pastor. Cross-references: Jer 2:2; Rev 3:15-16.

Verse 5

"Remember therefore from where you have fallen; repent, and do the works you did at first. If not, I will come to you and remove your lampstand from its place, unless you repent." (ESV)

Literal meaning: Jesus calls them to repent and return to their original works, or face judgment — removal of their church. SCJ reality (실상): The Ephesus pastor was warned to repent from his alliance with the political beast (Chun Doo-hwan's government backing Cheongji). When he did not, his "lampstand" (church) was removed in the 1980 betrayal, when the Jangmak Seongjeon pastoral system collapsed. Cross-references: Rev 2:16; Matt 21:43.

Verse 6

"Yet this you have: you hate the works of the Nicolaitans, which I also hate." (ESV)
